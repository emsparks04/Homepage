Reflections

Reflection 1 (Chapters 1-3)

After reading these chapters, what stuck out to me was how Spleak kinda acted as a language tool as well as emotional support. It made me think of ChatGPT and how some people use that a a resource. Personally, I use it for information and organization purposes. However, others might use it for more personal and emotional purposes. I also thought of a story I read in a previous DCD class where a women created a chat-box to mimic her friend that had passed away.

Reflection 2 (Chapters 4-5)

What stood out to me in this reading was the idea that websites look simple and creative, but they are actually very controlled. Most of them follow the same structure, which makes everything organized but also limits what can really be shared. Even when something seems random online, it usually still fits into a system. It made me think about how we present ourselves on social media or personal websites. We fit our lives into bios, categories, and posts that match the layout. In the end, it feels like we are in control, but the design is really shaping how we tell our story.

Reflection 3 (Chapters 6, 7, & 8)

This text really made me think about how connected our bodies, mins, and technology actually are. The idea that "there is no immateriality" stood out to me because it suggests that everything we do online still affects us physically and emotionally. Larry's story shows how constantly working and being "comppliant" can slowly erase someone's sense of identity, especially when their whole life revolves around screen and productivity. The shift from coding to creating BlockSmith felt like a breaking point where he finally tries to take control instead of just following instructions. Overall, the reading feels like a critique of hustle culture and digital life, showing how easy it is to lose yourself in systems that promise success but dont really offer meaning.

Reflection 4 (CSS Styling)

When reflecting on my experience in working with styling in CSS I acknowledge how much easier it has made this coding process. The style that is the most helpful for is the div class. This makes it much easier to code multiple things. It is helpful because it keeps my code more organized and requires less to type it out. I also dont have to repeat a code multiple times, instead i can just ise the div class. Other than that, I also enjoy being able to center and change the sizes of my images.
